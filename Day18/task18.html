Methodologies for Ontology Development

Overview

  • Several methodologies for ontology development:
    • Older Methodologies: Methontology, On-ToKnowledge, Ontology Development 101 (OD101).
    • Recent Methodologies: NeON, Melting Point, OntoSpec, DiDOn, TDDonto.

Key Differences

  • Micro-Level vs. Macro-Level:
    • Micro-Level: Focuses on formalization aspects (ontology authoring).
    • Macro-Level: Emphasizes processes from an information systems perspective.
  • Development Settings:
    • Older Methodologies: Single ontology by a few people in one location.
    • Newer Methodologies: Collaborative development with domain experts and knowledge engineers, allowing for distributed work.

Methodologies

Macro-Level Methodologies

  • Waterfall Approach: Steps include Specification, Conceptualization, Formalization, Implementation, and Maintenance.
  • Methontology: Structured process, can be superseded by NeON.
  • NeON: Offers multiple development scenarios; emphasizes Competency Questions (CQs) for ontology capabilities.

Lifecycle Model

  • Ontology Summit 2013: Provides a structured approach with questions to address at each stage (e.g., Requirements Development, Ontological Analysis, System Design).

Agile Approaches

  • Exploration of Agile-inspired methodologies, e.g., OntoMaven and eXtreme Design.

Micro-Level Methodologies

  • Examples include OntoSpec, OD101, and DiDOn, focusing on formalizing subject domains with guidelines from informal to logic-based representations.
