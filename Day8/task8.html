Google Search Overview

Google is the world’s most powerful search engine, allowing users to search its index of the web rather than the entire internet itself.

Advanced Google Search Commands

  • OR: Finds pages with one term or another (e.g., digital drawing OR digital painting).
  • Quotes (“ ”): Returns exact match results for phrases (e.g., “search term 1”).
  • Exclude (-): Excludes unwanted words from results (e.g., content marketing -businessinsider.com).
  • Add (+): Adds specific words to refine search results (e.g., content marketing +SEO).
  • Site:: Limits results to a specific site (e.g., site:example.com).
  • Related:: Finds websites similar to the specified domain (e.g., related:example.com).
  • Info:: Gathers information about a specific domain (e.g., info:example.com).

Search Operators

  • Cache: Shows the last cached version of a webpage (e.g., cache:example.com).
  • Allintext: Finds pages with all terms present in the text (e.g., allintext:social links).
  • Intext: Looks for terms anywhere on the page (e.g., word one intext:term).
  • Inposttitle: Finds blog posts with specific terms in the title (e.g., inposttitle:loss goals).
  • Allintitle: Finds pages with all terms in the title (e.g., allintitle:write content for SEO).
  • Intitle: Focuses on specific terms in the title (e.g., intitle:with micron pens).
  • Allinurl: Finds pages with specified terms in the URL (e.g., allinurl:drawing tablet).
  • Inurl: Looks for terms in the URL and content (e.g., inurl:portraits).
  • Allinanchor: Searches for terms in anchor text linking back to a page (e.g., allinanchor:"how to draw anime").
  • Inanchor: Identifies pages with inbound links containing specified anchor text (e.g., inanchor:"digital painting").
  • Filetype: Finds files of a specific type on a site (e.g., site:example.com filetype:pdf).
  • Around(): Narrows down results to terms that appear close to each other on the page (e.g., digital drawing AROUND(2) tools).

Combining Commands and Operators

Combining multiple commands can refine searches even further. For example, you can exclude multiple terms (e.g., term1 -term2 -term3) or search for results that exclude specific exact terms (e.g., technical SEO -"404 errors").

Technical SEO Audits

Use operators like site:example.com to identify indexing issues, find non-secure pages, audit http-to-https transitions, and locate duplicate content. Exclude subdomains or specific terms to focus on relevant content during technical SEO audits.

Combining Commands for Deep Research

Multiple combinations of operators can enhance research efficiency, whether you're hunting for plagiarism or auditing content for SEO purposes.
